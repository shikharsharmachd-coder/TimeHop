Foundations — 1943 to 1956
🧠
1943 Foundations
Artificial Neuron
Warren McCulloch and Walter Pitts published the first mathematical model of an artificial neuron. Their proof that logical operations could run through a neural network laid the bedrock of every AI system built since — from early perceptrons to today's transformers.

♟️
1950 Foundations
The Turing Test
Alan Turing asked history's most consequential question: can machines think? His Imitation Game test — where a machine must convince a judge it's human — still defines the philosophical frontier of AI research over 70 years later.
Read full deep dive →
🎓
1956 Foundations
Dartmouth Conference
John McCarthy, Marvin Minsky, and colleagues gathered for a summer workshop that officially coined the term "Artificial Intelligence." AI became a recognized scientific discipline — and a grand human ambition — on these grounds in New Hampshire.
Read full deep dive →
Early AI Systems — 1966 to 1986
💬
1966 Early Systems
ELIZA Chatbot
Joseph Weizenbaum's ELIZA was the first program to simulate human conversation through simple pattern matching. Some users genuinely believed they were talking to a real therapist — revealing just how easily humans anthropomorphize machines.
Read full deep dive →
❄️
1974 Early Systems
First AI Winter
Funding collapsed. Early AI systems failed to scale beyond toy problems, hardware was impossibly slow, and government bodies lost patience. The field entered a long freeze — forcing researchers to abandon hype in favour of mathematical rigour.
Read full deep dive →
⚙️
1980 Early Systems
Expert Systems
Rather than building general intelligence, AI pivoted to hyper-specific rule-based programs. MYCIN diagnosed blood infections with doctor-level accuracy. XCON configured computer orders, saving companies millions. AI found its first commercial home.
Read full deep dive →
📡
1986 Early Systems
Backpropagation
Rumelhart, Hinton, and Williams published the backpropagation algorithm — using calculus to let neural networks learn from their own mistakes by adjusting weights backwards through the network. Without this, modern deep learning simply would not exist.
Read full deep dive →
Breakthrough Era — 1997 to 2012
♟️
1997 Breakthroughs
Deep Blue
IBM's chess computer defeated world champion Garry Kasparov in a 6-game match, evaluating 200 million positions per second using custom VLSI chips. For the first time, a machine definitively surpassed a human at a task requiring complex strategic reasoning.
Read full deep dive →
🔬
2006 Breakthroughs
Deep Learning Revival
Geoffrey Hinton showed that deep belief networks could be trained layer-by-layer using contrastive divergence, solving the vanishing gradient problem that had blocked deep architectures for decades. The stage was set for the 2012 explosion that changed everything.
Read full deep dive →
🏆
2011 Breakthroughs
IBM Watson
Watson crushed human Jeopardy! champions on live television, demonstrating that machines could parse riddles, puns, and natural language nuance in milliseconds. It held 200 million pages of content in RAM — no internet connection required.
Read full deep dive →
💥
2012 Breakthroughs
The Deep Learning Boom
AlexNet slashed image recognition error from 26% to 15% at ImageNet, powered by NVIDIA GPUs and the ReLU activation function. The AI world permanently shifted — deep neural networks became the dominant paradigm for virtually every AI task.
Read full deep dive →
Modern AI Revolution — 2016 to 2022
⬡
2016 Modern AI
AlphaGo
DeepMind's AlphaGo defeated 18-time world Go champion Lee Sedol — a game with more positions than atoms in the universe. Its legendary "Move 37" was so unconventional it made Lee Sedol leave the room for 15 minutes in disbelief at what he'd just witnessed.
Read full deep dive →
📝
2020 Modern AI
Large Language Models
GPT-3 arrived with 175 billion parameters and demonstrated "few-shot learning" — writing essays, translating languages, and debugging code from just a short prompt. No fine-tuning needed. The AI landscape changed overnight and has never looked back.
Read full deep dive →
✨
2022 Modern AI
Generative AI Goes Mainstream
ChatGPT reached 100 million users in 2 months — the fastest product adoption in history. Midjourney and DALL-E made photorealistic art accessible to anyone with words. Every creative profession began reckoning with what AI-generated content truly meant.
